Orbito Privacy Policy

Last updated: February 18, 2026 In plain English (with the boring legal parts too).

The one-line version: We collect the minimum data needed to run the app, we never sell your location, and every location check is logged so you can see who peeked. That's the entire pitch.

Who we are

Orbito is a mobile app operated by Orbito Labs Ltd. ("Orbito," "we," "us," "our"). Contact: privacy@orbito.life. Website: orbito.life.

This Privacy Policy covers the Orbito mobile app (iOS, Android, web) and the orbito.life website. By using Orbito you agree to this policy. If you don't agree — don't sign in.

What we collect · and why

We collect only what we need to run the app. Here's every field, exactly:

1. Account data

  • Email address — to identify you and let you sign in via Google
  • Name — displayed to your Circle and Constellation members
  • Profile photo — shown as your avatar orb (from your Google account)
  • Google account ID — internal tie-in to your identity

Source: your Google account when you sign in with Google.

2. Location data

  • Precise GPS coordinates (latitude, longitude, speed, heading) — used to show your position on the map and to your Circle members
  • Approximate "home" coordinates — anchored the first time you broadcast, so we can render the map centered on you
  • Location history — the last 24 hours of breadcrumbs are retained; older data is purged

Source: your device's location services, only after you grant permission. Location is broadcast every ~15 seconds or 30 meters of movement, whichever comes first. Ghost Mode stops broadcasts instantly.

3. Activity data

  • Ledger entries — a log of every location check (yours and others') along with the reason given
  • Ping / SOS / Crash events — messages you send within Orbito
  • Trust Streak history — daily presence tracked per Circle relationship
  • Referral relationships — who invited whom (for cosmetic unlocks)

4. Payment data

  • Subscription plan and billing cycle — which tier you're on
  • Transaction history — created via Stripe (see "Third parties" below)
  • We do not store your credit card number. Stripe holds it.

5. Device & diagnostic data

  • Device model, OS version, timezone — for debugging
  • App version, screen size — for support
  • Crash reports — automatically collected when the app crashes
  • Battery level (when broadcasting) — used to display power-aware status

6. Data we DO NOT collect

  • ❌ We don't track your browsing outside Orbito
  • ❌ We don't scan your contacts, photos, messages, or calendar (unless you use a specific feature that opts in)
  • ❌ We don't have any advertising SDKs
  • ❌ We don't have any behavioral analytics SDKs that fingerprint you across apps

Your rights

You have the right to:

  • Access — see everything we have about you. Email privacy@orbito.life — we respond within 30 days.
  • Delete — permanently remove your account and all associated data. Do this yourself in the app (Profile → Settings → Delete Account) or email us. Data is deleted within 30 days of your request, except when we're legally required to retain (fraud, tax records).
  • Export — get a copy of your data in JSON. Email us.
  • Correct — fix any wrong data (edit your name, avatar) in the app or by emailing us.
  • Object — ask us to stop specific uses (like marketing emails — one-click unsubscribe in every email).
  • Restrict — pause processing while a complaint is investigated.
  • Complain — reach out to your local data protection authority. For EU: your national supervisory authority. For UK: ICO. For California: California AG.

For California residents (CCPA / CPRA)

You have the right to know, delete, correct, opt out of sale (we don't sell), limit use of sensitive personal info (like precise location), and not be discriminated against for exercising these rights. Requests: privacy@orbito.life.

How long we keep data

  • Account data: while your account is active
  • Location history: last 24 hours (older data auto-purged)
  • Ledger entries: while your account is active (users can delete individual entries)
  • Payment records: 7 years (tax / audit requirement)
  • Deleted accounts: all personal data purged within 30 days; anonymized aggregate stats may remain

Location data · special notes

Because location data is the whole product:

  • We only collect it when the app is in the foreground (there's no background tracking by default — that would require a native build we haven't shipped).
  • You can stop broadcasting instantly via Ghost Mode.
  • Ghost Mode is transparent — your Circle sees "Going ghost 👻," not a fake location.
  • Location is NEVER used for advertising, cross-app tracking, or sold in any form.
  • We retain only the last 24h of breadcrumbs. Everything older is auto-purged.

Children

Orbito is not intended for children under 13 years old (or under 16 in the EU). We do not knowingly collect data from users below these ages. If you believe a child has signed up, email privacy@orbito.life and we'll delete the account.

If a parent adds a child (13+) to their Circle: the child needs their own Orbito account with their own consent. There is no "parental view" or "supervised mode" today — every user has equal rights on the platform.

Cookies · web only

The web version of Orbito uses strictly-necessary cookies only — one for your login session, one to remember your consent choices. No analytics or advertising cookies.

Security

We take reasonable steps to protect your data: - All traffic in and out of Orbito uses TLS 1.2+ - Passwords are never stored (we use Google Sign-In instead) - Location data is encrypted at rest in MongoDB Atlas - Payment data lives at Stripe (PCI-DSS Level 1)

No system is perfectly secure. If we discover a breach that materially affects you, we'll notify you within 72 hours of confirming it.

Changes to this policy

If we materially change this policy, we'll: 1. Update the "Last updated" date at the top 2. Notify all users by email 30 days before the change takes effect 3. Show an in-app banner asking you to re-consent (if the change is substantive)

Minor edits (fixing typos, clarifying wording) may go live without notice.
